Steak Menu

Basted Rib-Eye

This steak is based on a recipe from chef Alain Ducasse. Halfway through cooking, these bone-in rib eyes are basted with a mixture of butter, thyme, and garlic, so they're crusty outside and richly flavored.

Porter House Steak

For a perfectly tender and crusty steak, the chefs salt their meat up to 12 hours before and let it sit, uncovered, in the refrigerator. This step seasons the meat to its core and pulls out moisture for a better sear.

Steak Au Poivre

The well-marbled cut stays more tender than New York strip, and its rich, beefy flavor infuses the pan sauce. Trim the steak of large pieces of fat and tie it into a round for even cooking.
